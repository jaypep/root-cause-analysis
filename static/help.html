Getting started

Follow these steps the first time you set up the app:

  • 1. Settings first — enter your weather station ID, latitude, longitude, and frost dates. Without frost dates, planting tasks won't generate.
  • 2. Set up your structure — go to Manage Garden and add your zones, beds, and containers so you have places to put plants.
  • 3. Add crops — crops are the varieties you grow. The app ships with common ones, but you can add custom varieties in Manage Garden → Crops.
  • 4. Add seeds to inventory — go to Seeds and log what you have on hand, including the lot year for viability tracking.
  • 5. Sync planting tasks — hit "Sync planting tasks" on the Tasks page or dashboard to generate your sowing and transplant schedule from your seed inventory and frost dates.
  • 6. Add plants — as you sow or transplant, log them on the Plants page and assign them to a bed or container.
Auto-generated tasks

The app generates two kinds of tasks automatically:

Water garden — created when your weather station reports dry conditions (no significant rainfall). Completing it marks it done for 24 hours before it can reappear.
Planting tasks — Sow Indoors, Sow Outdoors, and Transplant tasks generated from your seed inventory. Dates are calculated from your last spring frost and first fall frost dates in Settings.

Transplant crops (those with weeks-to-transplant set) get a Sow Indoors task and a Transplant task. Direct-sow crops get a Sow Outdoors task timed relative to last frost using their direct_sow_weeks offset.

For crops with succession sowing configured, multiple tasks are created spaced by the succession interval.

Frost dates and growing zone

Go to Settings → Garden Planning to set:

  • Last spring frost — the average date of the final frost in spring. Used to schedule indoor sowing and transplanting.
  • First fall frost — used to schedule fall succession sowing, counting backward from harvest time.
  • USDA growing zone — used for general plant hardiness reference. The app can detect this automatically from your coordinates.
Tip: If planting tasks aren't appearing, check that your frost dates are saved. The Save button on the Garden Planning tab saves independently from the Weather tab.
Seed viability

Each seed entry shows a viability badge based on lot year and the crop's expected viability window:

  • Green (e.g. 1/3 yr) — well within viability window
  • Amber (aging) — within 1 year of the viability limit; use soon or test germination
  • Red (expired) — past the expected viability window
  • — (dash) — no lot year recorded

Use the Low viability tab on the Seeds page to quickly see which packets need attention before the next sowing season.

Managing crops and varieties

Crops live in Manage Garden → Crops. Each entry can have:

  • Days to harvest — used for fall succession task scheduling
  • Weeks to transplant — if set, this crop gets Sow Indoors + Transplant tasks (not Sow Outdoors)
  • Direct sow weeks — signed offset from last spring frost (negative = before frost, positive = after). Overrides the built-in default. Useful for overwintering crops like Purple Sprouting broccoli or garlic.
  • Succession weeks / count — generates multiple staggered sowing tasks
Overwintering crops like garlic or Purple Sprouting broccoli need a large negative direct_sow_weeks value (e.g. -20 to -26) to schedule sowing in autumn rather than spring.
